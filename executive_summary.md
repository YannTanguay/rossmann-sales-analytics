# Executive Summary  
## Rossmann Retail Sales Performance Analysis

This analysis uses historical retail sales data to identify the key drivers of sales and support data-informed management decisions.  SQL, multiple regression analysis and decision-tree modelling are used to evaluate the affect of customer traffic, promotional activity, store characteristics, and seasonal trends on sales performance.

---

## Key Business Insights

**Customer Traffic as Primary Revenue Driver**  
Volume of customers is found to be the strongest driver of daily store sales, with regression results showing that each additional customer contributes to roughly €7.38 in daily sales, on average.  This suggests that strategies focusing in increasing customer traffic are fundamental to improving sales performance.

**Promotions Significantly Increase Sales Performance**  
Promotions are correlated with higher daily sales and higher customer volume.  Decision tree analysis shows that running promotions increases the probability of seeing high-sales days and has an incremental benefit of rough €869, which suggests that promotional expenses are justified when costs can stay below this break-even threshold.

**Store Format and Product Assortment Influence Revenue Potential**  
Sales performance varies between types of stores and product assortments, suggesting that certain structural retail characteristics have a meaningful affect on sales numbers. This supports more targeted operational planning as well as localisation of strategy.

**Seasonal Demand Patterns Affect Sales Stability**  
Monthly sales trends show there are recurring fluctuations in sales, with better performance during later months of the year.  Understanding of seasonal patterns can further support in operation planning for things like staffing levels and timing of promotion campaigns.

**Competitive Environment Impacts Store Outcomes**  
Stores with a greater distance from competition show marginally higher sales, suggesting that local market environment can affect sales potential, and should be factored into store location decisions.

---

## Managerial Implications
- Initiatives that support customer traffic in stores should be prioritised; this includes localised marketing and more targeted promotional campaigns.
- Promotions should be implemented selectively to ensure that incremental sales benefit exceeds promotion costs.
- Store format and product assortment decisions should be aligned with local demands to optimise sales potential.
- Seasonal demand forecasting should be incorporated to support inventory management and staffing decisions.
- Proximity to competitors should be factored in when evaluating store performance and expansion strategies.

---

## Conclusion

This analysis shows how integration of data analytics techniques, including SQL-based exploratory analysis, decision tree modelling and regression analysis, can provide actionable insights into optimising retail sales performance.  These findings can support decision-making related to strategy, promotion planning, resource allocation, and long-run management for retail performance.
